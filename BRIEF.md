# Design Brief: Sebastian van Eickelen Portfolio

Source-of-truth governing document for all design, motion, spacing, typography, and implementation decisions.

---

## Core Positioning

This website should position Sebastian van Eickelen as a premium filmmaker and photographer through structure, pacing, and taste rather than through generic filmmaker tropes. The visual lane is not a standard reel-first portfolio. It is a Swiss editorial portfolio with cinematic media used selectively, restrained motion, and strong typographic hierarchy.

The site should feel like the meeting point between a high-production photography monograph and a disciplined Swiss design system.

The benchmark is not other filmmaker portfolios. It is the overlap between high-end editorial design, premium creative studio sites, and photography monograph logic.

---

## Visual Lane

Swiss editorial structure + cinematic media + restrained scroll choreography + full-screen desktop scene composition.

---

## What the Site Should Feel

- premium
- editorial
- cinematic
- restrained
- structured
- current
- calm
- intentional

## What the Site Should Not Feel

- like a template
- like a standard filmmaker reel site
- like a dark SaaS landing page
- like a graphic design studio site with film added on top
- like a motion showcase doing tricks for attention

---

## Design Principles

1. Swiss grid logic as foundation
2. Cinematic warmth layered on top of that structure
3. Wide desktop canvas with disciplined content zones inside it
4. Strong but restrained typography
5. Minimal, muted surfaces with one controlled accent
6. One major visual argument per scene
7. Clear spacing, safe zones, and editorial rhythm
8. Motion only where it improves hierarchy, depth, or pacing

The grid is the skeleton. Typography is the main hierarchy tool. Media is punctuation and authority, not wallpaper. Motion is pacing, not spectacle.

---

## Palette Direction

- near-black
- paper / off-white
- muted neutral greys
- one restrained cinematic accent
- no loud color system
- no decorative color noise

---

## Typography Direction

- structural, not decorative
- maximum of two font families
- hierarchy through scale, weight, spacing, and role
- opening type moment should feel like a directorial statement
- metadata, utilities, and labels should be controlled and precise
- use exact fonts and weights from Figma where available

---

## Surface Language

Minimalism should not mean flatness. Use controlled material richness where useful:

- subtle texture or grain
- slight tonal depth
- muted surface variation
- premium restraint

Do not add effects for their own sake. Use surface treatment to create emotional depth, not clutter.

---

## Layout Philosophy

The site should behave like a sequence of editorial scenes, not stacked utility modules. Use a wide full-width canvas for surfaces, media, and structural bands. Keep text measures, metadata, and form widths controlled.

### Desktop Scene Structure

1. Hero — 100vh
2. Selected Work — 100vh
3. About + Worked With — 100vh
4. Services — 100vh
5. Cinematic section — 100vh
6. Contact + Footer — 100vh

Each scene needs:
- top safe zone
- main content zone
- lower support zone
- balanced internal anchors

---

## Section Principles

### Hero
- 100vh, editorial opening scene
- directorial statement
- strong internal composition
- if video is used, typography and structure must still dominate
- do not let the video replace design judgment

### Selected Work
- one of the strongest sections
- archive/list + preview logic is the spine of the homepage
- must remain curated, editorial, and distinctive
- should feel like a full-screen scene, not a compact module

### Worked With
- not a standalone major scene
- sits inside About + proof logic
- feels like a credibility annotation, not a startup logo strip
- title treatment should be correct and quiet

### About
- full-screen editorial portrait/positioning scene
- calm, premium, and human
- answers who Sebastian is, where he is based, and what kind of work he does
- should not feel like biography clutter

### Services
- full-screen scene
- must not read like a commodity services menu
- frames Sebastian as a premium creative partner
- service taxonomy must stay at a consistent strategic level
- service plates should feel integrated into the design language, not like generic UI cards

### Cinematic Section
- full-screen scene
- one strong emotional/media moment
- supports looped media and restrained parallax
- CTA card can be larger, clearer, and more persuasive
- major atmospheric bridge before Contact

### Contact
- full-screen closing scene with Footer integrated
- final invitation, not a footer with inputs
- must include a reduced premium inquiry form
- email, address, form, and send action clearly grouped
- enough breathing room to feel expensive and calm

---

## Contact Form Philosophy

Keep the form. The right solution is an editorial contact section with a reduced, elegant inquiry form.

**Fields:** Name, Email, Project / Brief

**Style:** minimal, line-based, thin rules, generous spacing, subtle focus states — no heavy boxes, no clunky default UI.

---

## Services Taxonomy

All service labels must sit at a coherent premium strategic level. Do not mix strategic labels with overly executional labels in a way that lowers the whole section.

---

## Motion Principles

Motion should feel like film pacing and editorial sequencing. Do not use motion for spectacle.

**Allowed motion:**
- load reveals
- section reveals
- masked text reveals
- subtle media transitions
- restrained parallax in the cinematic section
- refined hover cues
- elegant form focus states
- project preview transitions

**Avoid:**
- heavy glassmorphism as a dominant system
- bento layouts
- WebGL spectacle without a reason
- exaggerated parallax
- overactive hover behavior
- springy UI motion
- hero loops that behave like generic wallpaper

---

## Media Strategy

Media should be selective and load-bearing.

**Hero:** may use a looping video background. If used, it must be tightly curated and subordinate to the composition. The hero must read as an art-directed opening scene.

**Cinematic section:** strong emotional scene. Can use looped video or moving portrait. Supports restrained parallax. One of the site's most important atmospheric moments.

---

## What to Borrow

- strict grid discipline
- typography-led hierarchy
- editorial pacing
- neutral and muted palette systems
- list-plus-preview archive logic
- work-first structure
- selective cinematic media
- restrained motion
- strong whitespace as structure

## What to Ban

- generic filmmaker hero-video-plus-text formulas
- dark mode as a default personality
- bento-grid layout behavior
- glassmorphism as the main aesthetic
- decorative gradients without purpose
- noisy parallax
- WebGL spectacle without conceptual justification
- generic service cards
- UI patterns borrowed from product marketing
- any implementation that feels like a template instead of an authored portfolio

---

## Implementation Rules

**Design-accurate, not just engineering-clean.** Use the Figma as the visual source of truth. Use actual Figma values for fonts, spacing, dimensions, and proportions where available. Do not build from screenshots alone. Do not approximate if the design file can be inspected.

**No redesign for the sake of novelty.** No random new sections. No trend-chasing effects. Every decision must strengthen one of these:
- hierarchy
- pacing
- clarity
- authority
- atmosphere
- conversion

---

## Final Lane Definition

This website belongs at the intersection of Swiss editorial structure and cinematic image authority. The governing logic is a Müller-Brockmann-style grid used for proportion, spacing, and hierarchy, combined with the warmth and atmosphere of a filmmaker who works in light, movement, and texture. The experience should feel like reading a high-production photography monograph designed by someone who also directs films.
