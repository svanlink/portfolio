# Strategy Review Prompt

---

## Template

```
Read BRIEF.md in full.

Review [SECTION / WHOLE SITE] against the strategic positioning in BRIEF.md.

The strategic test is this: does the site belong at the intersection of
Swiss editorial structure and cinematic image authority? Would it benchmark
against high-end editorial design, premium creative studio sites, and
photography monograph logic — or does it read as a filmmaker template?

Evaluate:

1. Scene sequence: do the 6 scenes (Hero, Selected Work, About + Worked With,
   Services, Cinematic, Contact + Footer) tell a coherent story about who
   Sebastian is and why his work is worth hiring?

2. Section purpose: does each section do exactly one thing — and does that
   thing serve the positioning? Flag any section that feels redundant,
   misplaced, or lower-status than the others.

3. Services positioning: do the service labels read at a consistent premium
   strategic level, or does any label sound executional? (Per BRIEF.md:
   "Do not mix strategic labels with overly executional labels in a way
   that lowers the whole section.")

4. Ban list check: identify any element, motion, or layout pattern from the
   BRIEF.md ban list that is present in the current implementation.

5. Conversion logic: does the Contact scene function as a final invitation,
   or does it read as a generic footer with inputs? Does it have enough
   breathing room?

Output a verdict per item above: PASS, NEEDS WORK, or CRITICAL. For each
NEEDS WORK or CRITICAL, propose the specific change.
```
